Phosphotyrosine-Inducible Protein Switches (2024–2025)
I designed binders for a phosphorylation-inducible protein switch using an iterative LigandMPNN and FastRelax pipeline, then filtered roughly 80,000 candidate designs on prediction confidence (pLDDT, ipTM, PAE), RMSD, contacts and clashes, hydrogen bonds, shape complementarity, interface energy, and ddG. I supported experimental validation through cloning, expression, Strep-Tactin purification, SEC, and gel electrophoresis, and presented the work at the UW Undergraduate Research Symposium.
LDL-Targeting Binder Strategy (2025–2026)
I contributed to designing LDL-targeting binders for a degradation strategy, and supported the experimental validation through protein purification, yeast workflows, and flow-cytometry-based binding assays. I helped interpret the assay data to guide the next design round.
BRD4-Targeted Binder Design (2026, ongoing)
I work on binder design against BRD4, an epigenetic regulator and oncology-relevant target. I generate and filter candidate binders against the target surface and prepare promising designs for experimental testing.

CCEP Artifact Rejection: Capstone & WRF Project (2025–2026)
I designed the stimulation protocols, collected multi-channel micro-ECoG recordings from rat cortex, and built the Python pipeline for trial alignment, baseline correction, filtering, artifact-template reconstruction and subtraction, visualization, response comparison, and electrode-level quality control. I am extending it toward near real-time processing, using model-based and machine-learning-informed approaches to reconstruct artifact templates for closed-loop neuromodulation research.
Hebbian Plasticity & Chemogenetic Facilitation (2024–2025)
Supporting a senior-led study of chemogenetic disinhibition and paired stimulation in rat somatosensory cortex, I was lead surgeon on 5 of 30+ procedures. Certified in three rodent surgical protocols, I ran viral injections, physiological monitoring, tissue processing, and fluorescence-based verification of viral expression.
Spike Sorting & Neural Decoding
I evaluated Kilosort4 spike-sorting for the chemogenetic project. In parallel coursework, I built a multi-unit activity pipeline (MAD-based spike thresholding with raster and PSTH analysis) to compare neural entrainment across constant, 3 Hz, and 10 Hz stimulation.

About

Systems Engineering for Biological & Neural Research

I am a University of Washington Bioengineering and Biochemistry Honors student with a Data Science option, graduating in August 2026. My work sits in research environments where experiments, computation, and engineering all have to hold together. I have worked across neural engineering, protein design, electrophysiology, signal processing, computational biology, wet-lab validation, and data analysis. The common thread is not a single biological scale or disease area. It is building careful, usable systems for difficult biological questions.

In the Neural Engineering, Repair & Design Lab, I lead a Washington Research Foundation Fellowship project on stimulation-evoked neural responses, artifact rejection, and near real-time processing for closed-loop neuromodulation research. I designed stimulation protocols, collected and analyzed multi-channel micro-ECoG recordings from rat cortex, and built Python pipelines for trial alignment, filtering, baseline correction, artifact-template reconstruction, subtraction, visualization, response comparison, and electrode-level quality control. That work trained me to deal with messy biological signals, hardware constraints, experimental variability, and analysis decisions where small details can change the interpretation.

At the Institute for Protein Design (Baker Lab), I work on computational and experimental protein design. My projects have included phosphorylation-inducible protein switches, LDL-targeting binder strategies, and BRD4-targeted binder design. I have used RFdiffusion, LigandMPNN, Rosetta/FastRelax, AlphaFold/Chai-1, Python, and Bash to generate, organize, and filter large candidate sets. I have also supported experimental validation through cloning, expression, protein purification, SEC, gel electrophoresis, yeast workflows, flow-cytometry-based binding assays, and assay-data interpretation.

Across these projects, I am strongest when the problem is not already clean. I can write analysis code, interpret biological data, troubleshoot experimental systems, document methods, and communicate results clearly. I am looking for research roles where I can contribute as a hands-on early-career researcher who learns quickly, works carefully, and builds tools that make biological data more reliable and useful.
